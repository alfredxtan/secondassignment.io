Digital Piano


Worried that an Acoustic Piano may be too much for you? You can consider a Digital Piano instead! With the same 88 keys, you get the full range of sounds like the acoustic piano for as little as 10% of the price! An average digital piano could cost you anywhere from $500 to 2000 dollars. Although it may still be costly, it is a much affordable alternative!

The sounds of the piano are pre-recorded during manufacturing. This differs from its acoustic counterpart, which has a hammer mechanism. Nevertheless, because the sounds are pre-recorded, there may be a range of other pre-recorded sounds, such as Cello and Violin!

What's more? Some models allow you to record your own playing for you to analyze your playing more carefully! Yet another major difference is the convenience of the digital piano: You do not have to call in a tuner regularly unlike the acoustic ones, and you can even bring the piano around in some instances!

You might then ask: Why would anyone purchase an acoustic piano?

First, the touch of the digital piano differs from that of the acoustic ones. Therefore, if you intend to pursue piano seriously, I suggest you get an upright piano for yourself (perhaps a second-hand one!) Also, some people really like the sound of an actual piano, as opposed to a recording played from the digital one.
