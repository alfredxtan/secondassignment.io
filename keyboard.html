Keyboard


The keyboard is yet the cheapest alternative, costing between $100 and $1000. Like the digital piano, the sounds are pre-recorded. However, the difference lies with the number of keys available on a keyboard -- there are usually 66 keys, with some models having slightly more with 72 or 88 keys. This means that an entry-level keyboard may not have the range of sounds offered by the digital and acoustic piano.

Furthermore, just like the digital piano, the keyboard requires no tuning and usually comes with a wider variety of pre-recorded sounds as compared to the digital ones. Furthermore, it is portable, allowing you to bring along for a gig if need be!
