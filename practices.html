Practices


✋Getting Your Hands Dirty✋

When it comes to learning an instrument, practices are very important. Much like a skill, it takes many hours of practices for you to finally master an instrument. In this section, we will go through some of the procedures for you to get started on your practices!


Warm-ups

Warming up is an essential part of practicing: You have to get your fingers agile before taking on a challenging piece, otherwise you might risk them not being as fluid as you hope.

    🚨 We would like to share some tips for practicing the piano before we delve into the practices themselves:
  1. Set aside at least 20 minutes each day to practice

    2. Practice makes perfect; same goes for learning the piano. Spend sufficient time everyday (20 mins per day is recommended) to work your fingers!

  2. Don't forget to warm up

    3. It is important for you to warm up your hands for them to move fluidly! You can refer back to learn some of the warm-ups!

  3. Don't try to take on too much

    4. It takes time and patience to perfect a particular song. Set your goals for each session and try to achieve them, but do not strive to over-achieve!

  4. Practice slowly

    5. Rushing through a piece means that you might make mistakes. This can get frustrating, and even worse, you might pick up those mistakes. Play each note deliberately and slowly!

  5. Pick a piece that you want to play

    6. Nothing would motivate you more than picking your favourite piece to practice! You get to both practice while also enjoy the beautiful melody!
